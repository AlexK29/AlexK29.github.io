Our Topics

Political Concepts
Understood and discussed, impartially.

In this day and age, it is all too easy to make conclusive arguments on policies with very little understanding. People find themselves defending sets of values only because they derived from an organisation or a person they support or admire.
Yet with grounding in specific political ideas, readers can consider both the benefits and the consequences of situations, and make objective decisions about their standpoint irrespective of exterior motives.

Perspectives
Take a look into someone else's mind.

Fulfilment from life is something individuals explore in their own ways, and exposing various forms of it can indeed provide inspiration and intrigue to the reader.
We, therefore, aim to showcase a diverse array of people with different objectives and the circumstances each encountered and dealt with.

World Affairs
Our world changes with us.

Often we find ourselves neglectful of international affairs mainly due to a relative insignificance to personal problems. However, with mass migration a consistent theme in modern politics in both the UK and USA, the crises of the world are increasingly relevant to developed countries such as these.
In a world that is ever-more connected and interdependent, changes and occurrences across seas have become increasingly important and impactful. For this reason, we find it essential to dedicate space on our publication for world affairs.
